## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Global defense spending is experiencing an unprecedented multi-year expansion driven by geopolitical tensions, NATO commitments, and technological modernization. This analysis identifies **Defense Spending Surge** as the primary trend chain with 5 key sub-trends, mapping the value chain from R&D through government contracts, with particular focus on Canadian companies positioned to benefit from domestic procurement increases.

**Key Data Points (as of March 2026)**:
- NATO 2% GDP target now universal; 5% target adopted for 2035
- Canada: Committed to 2% by March 2026, $585.9B industrial strategy
- US Defense Budget: ~$886B FY2026, focused on missiles, cyber, space
- Drone market: Expected to reach $57B by 2030 (18% CAGR)

---

## 2. SUB-TRENDS (5 Key Pillars)

### SUB-TREND 2A: NATO & Allied Spending Convergence

**Description**: Historic convergence toward 2% NATO target with 5% aspirational target for 2035

**Key Data**:
- All 32 NATO members now committed to 2% minimum
- 23 countries currently meeting or exceeding 2%
- Canada: 1.76% (2025) → 2% (March 2026) → 5% (2035 target)
- Germany: Major increase to 2.1% GDP
- Poland: Leading at 4%+ GDP

**Investment Implication**: Broad-based Western defense spending increase, not US-only

---

### SUB-TREND 2B: NORAD Modernization & Arctic Defense

**Description**: North American continental defense modernization

**Key Programs**:
- NORAD modernization: $38B over 20 years (Canada)
- Arctic domain awareness: Satellite, radar, undersea systems
- Operation NANOOK-NUNALIVUT 2026: Largest Canadian Arctic exercise

**Canadian Companies**: MDA Space, Calian Group, Kraken Robotics

---

### SUB-TREND 2C: Drone & Autonomous Systems Proliferation

**Description**: Ukraine war demonstrated drone dominance; all militaries modernizing

**Market Size**:
- Global drone defense market: $57B by 2030 (18% CAGR)
- Loitering munitions: $8B by 2028
- US drone procurement: $10B+ annually

**Key Segments**:
- ISR drones (reconnaissance)
- Loitering munitions (suicide drones)
- Autonomous systems
- Counter-drone technology

**Second-Order**: Components (sensors, communications, batteries) benefit regardless of which drone platform wins

---

### SUB-TREND 2D: Missile Defense & Precision Strike

**Description**: Missile defense systems and precision-guided munitions

**Key Programs**:
- US: THAAD, Patriot, Iron Dome (US deployment)
- NATO: Integrated air and missile defense
- Hypersonic weapons defense: Emerging priority

**Canadian Exposure**: Limited direct participation but supply chain beneficiaries

---

### SUB-TREND 2E: Cybersecurity & Hybrid Warfare

**Description**: Modern warfare includes significant cyber component

**Market Size**:
- Global defense cybersecurity: $30B+ by 2028
- Critical infrastructure protection: Major focus
- Zero-trust architecture: Required for defense

**Canadian Companies**: BlackBerry, Trend Micro (local), Calian (government services)

## CONCLUSION

The Defense Spending Surge represents a **secular, multi-year trend** supported by:
- Binding NATO commitments (2% → 5%)
- Canadian domestic procurement targets (70%)
- Geopolitical tailwinds (Ukraine, Taiwan, Arctic)
- Technological modernization requirements

Canadian companies have meaningful exposure through:
- Established defense industrial base
- NORAD/Arctic defense focus
- Dual-use technology expertise (space, cybersecurity)

**Recommended Approach**: Core positions in CAE and MDA Space, satellite positions in Magellan and Calian, with global primes for diversification.
