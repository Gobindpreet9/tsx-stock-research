## CHAIN 4: U.S. Trade Realignment & Allied Reshoring

**CHAIN:** US Tariff Policy → North American Supply Chain Restructuring → Canadian Manufacturing Advantage

**CATALYST:** USMCA review deadline July 1, 2026. U.S. Trade Representative Jamieson Greer stated "any trade deal with Canada will include tariffs" (Feb 2026). Canada currently faces 10% non-compliant duties, 50% steel/aluminum tariffs, 25% automotive tariffs. Trump administration seeking "fundamental rewrite" with stricter rules of origin.

**MACRO ALIGNMENT:** NEUTRAL-SLIGHTLY FAVORED
- Tariff pressure creates near-term margin compression
- BUT: Canada viewed as lowest-tariff alternative relative to other countries
- Rules of origin changes benefit Canadian manufacturing (vs. transshipment)
- Auto/steel reshoring creates opportunities

**GRAPH:**
```
US Tariff Threats → USMCA Renegotiation → Stricter Rules of Origin → Canadian Manufacturing Reshoring → Industrial Demand
```

**STAGE:** EARLY (Review underway, negotiations beginning)

**CANADIAN EDGE:** MIXED

- "Near-shoring" alternative to Asia
- Established auto/steel supply chains
- BUT: Tariffs still create headwinds

**PRICED IN:**
